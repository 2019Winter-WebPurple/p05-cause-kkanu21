The Immigration & Refugee Crisis: A Global Emergency

Handling Refugees in the US

Dating back to early years, refugees were welcomed into the United States under the International Humanitarian Law. Since 1980, the refugee program began to fluctuate. The large civil war in Syria resulted in a large mugration crisis across the world, creating a need to strengthen the US immigration program. Instead, President Donald Trump recently lowered the number of refugee acceptance in the United States. To ensure the safety of millions, there will need to be change.

Effects - The Refugee Crisis

The consistent brutal combat and hunger in Middle Eastern and African nations has forced families to flee from their homes. These different obstacles result in an unsafe, insufficient ara to raise children. Many are often exposed to disease child labor child marriage/abuse and lack of education.

How to Help

Immigration is a very difficult process for millions, especially refugees. It is hard for families to find new homes. It is very important to get involved in a cause that you are passionate about. There are several ways that you can help.

  1. Get Educated - It is important that you know what is going on in the world around you.
  2. Speak up - Fight for what you believe in and try to help educate others.
  3. Donate - Give money to help families to find a home and get back on their feet. Give now!
